# Roadmap

This semester runs one onboarding sprint and four regular sprints (3 weeks each). We are a team of five — and in every sprint, each person delivers a complete, demo‑ready prototype from idea to reset procedure. That’s 5 prototypes × 4 sprints = 20 finished prototypes by the end of the semester.

!!! tip "Use the definitions when planning"
Before you start, check: [Definition of Ready](definition-of-ready.md) → After you finish, verify: [Definition of Done](definition-of-done.md)

---

## Onboarding sprint (3 weeks)
- Goal: get tooling ready (MkDocs site, repo, theme), agree on build standards, and produce a tiny first demo
- Deliverable: a simple working feature stub (e.g., a blinking traffic light on a breadboard) documented with the template

Suggested quick wins (pick one each):
- Blink an LED with a clean reset procedure
- Read a sensor reliably (debounced button, IR, ultrasonic) and log it
- Move a servo/motor safely within limits

---

## How each sprint works (for prototypes)
- Each person picks one concrete idea and ships a working prototype in 3 weeks
- Follow build standards so the prototype fits city style and wiring
- Prefer local/standalone operation first; integrations can come later
- Document with the [Feature template](features/_template.md) and include a reset procedure
- Aim for 10× consecutive demo runs without manual fixes
