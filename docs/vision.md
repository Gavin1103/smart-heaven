# Vision: Smart Heaven

Smart Heaven is our learning group’s playful take on a solarpunk city: bright, green, and human‑centered. We combine physical making (3D printing, laser cutting) with smart behavior (sensors, lights, motors, data) so that every sprint delivers a working, demo‑ready slice of the city.

## What we’re building
- A modular city on plywood tiles of 30×30×0.36 cm
- Laser‑engraved roads (straight, curve, T‑split, crossing, bridge, railway)
- Smart features you can see and feel: traffic lights, a bridge, a railway barrier, parking, and more
- A simple data backbone (MQTT/HTTP + dashboard) so features can react to sensors and each other

## Why it matters
- Learn by doing: embedded/robotics + backend work together on the same artifact
- Practice standards: mechanics fit, wiring is safe and labeled, software predictable
- Demo quality: every sprint ends with something we can show confidently

## Two routes, one city
- Embedded & Robotics: sensors, actuators, safety, physical reliability, wiring
- Backend: messaging (MQTT/HTTP), services, storage, dashboards, simple UIs

We integrate early and often. Backend can simulate sensors when needed; embedded can run locally without the network. But by the sprint review, the two should connect.

## Definition of Done (per sprint)
- Works 10× in a row without manual fixing
- Has a clear reset procedure (documented on the feature page)
- Follows build standards (tile, electronics, software)
- Documented with photos/gifs and short notes on decisions and risks

## North star
By the end of the project, observers can look around Smart Heaven and see several features coordinating: traffic pauses for a train, a bridge announces and blocks the road, parking shows live availability, and the dashboard reflects the city’s state in real time.

## Stakeholders
- Two teachers act as the city’s mayors and final decision makers. Check in with them for approvals on standards, protocols, and demo scope.
